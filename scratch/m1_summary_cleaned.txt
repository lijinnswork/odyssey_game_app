As we come to the end of module one, let's take a moment to step back
and reflect on what we've covered.
We began by demystifying artificial intelligence
to understand what AI actually is, how it
works at a basic level, and where it already shows up
in our everyday lives and classrooms.
We explored the evolution of technology and the
history of AI, tracing its journey from early
rule-based systems to today's data-driven
generative models. This taught us that AI progress
has always moved in cycles of excitement,
limitations, and reinvention. We then looked ahead,
discussing what the next decade of AI could mean for education.
More personalization, better learning support, and new
possibilities alongside real challenges that demand thoughtful
choices from educators. A key focus throughout this module has been
relevance, why AI matters for educators today, how it's
being used in teaching and learning, how students are already
engaging with it, and how faculty can guide this shift
rather than react to it. We also emphasized
responsible and ethical use understanding AI's
limitations, being alert to bias, protecting data
privacy, and remembering that professional
judgment always rests with the educator.
Finally, through terminology, use cases, and your
assignment, the goal has been to build confidence,
not mastery of tools, but clarity of
thinking.
